This week, our group started to move towards one final topic. Before this, we had several ideas, but we needed to choose one and make it more specific. I think this was an important step because if the topic is still unclear, it is very hard to decide the functions of the website.

We looked again at the student support idea. I still think it is useful, but it feels too close to tools that already exist. Also, students already have many online spaces to communicate. It would be hard for our website to be very different unless we developed a very specific feature, but we did not have a strong enough idea for that.

We also looked again at the local community idea. I liked this idea at first because it could include many useful things. But this was also the problem. It had too many possible directions. If we tried to include events, shopping, second-hand items, and local news, the website would become too complicated. If we only picked one part, then the website might feel unfinished.

Because of this, we became more interested in the middle-aged and older adult social website. The target users are clearer, and the main function is also clearer. The website is mainly for social connection. Users can share their daily life, read other people’s posts, comment, and maybe learn useful life tips.

At first, we imagined the website could be like TikTok, where users share daily life. But later we realised that making a video-based platform would be too difficult for our prototype. It may also not be the best choice for the users we are focusing on. So we changed the idea to a simpler format. Users can share text posts, maybe images, and short life tips. This is easier to build and easier to use.

Another function we discussed was coupons or platform benefits. For example, the website could show supermarket discounts or special offers for older users. I think this is a useful idea because it connects the website to daily life, not only online communication.

By the end of this week, I felt our project direction became much clearer. We are not trying to build a huge social media app. We are trying to build a simple and friendly community website for middle-aged and older adults.
