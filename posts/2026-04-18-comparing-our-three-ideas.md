This week, I started to think more carefully about the three ideas our group discussed. At first, I only thought about whether the idea was interesting. But later I realised that for A2, the idea also needs to be possible to build as a prototype. We cannot make a very large website with too many different functions.

The student support website was the easiest idea for us to understand because we are students. We know the problems students have, such as finding assignment information, managing deadlines, and asking classmates for help. But this idea also had a problem. Many of these things are already done by Canvas, university websites, or group chats. If our website only repeats these functions, it may not have a strong reason to exist.

The local community website sounded useful, but it was hard to control. It could include local events, second-hand trading, supermarket discounts, local recommendations, or neighbourhood help. These functions are all different, and each one needs its own user flow and database information. I felt this idea might become too big for our group, especially because we still need to build the actual website and not only design it.

The middle-aged and older adult social website became more interesting to me. Its main purpose is simple: helping users connect with others. From this purpose, we can design several related functions. For example, users can post about their daily life, comment on other people’s posts, share life tips, and check some useful coupons. These functions are different, but they still connect to one main idea: making daily life more social and useful.

However, this idea also needs careful thinking. We should not assume all older users are the same. Some people may be good at using technology, while others may need a very simple interface. So the website should not be too crowded. The text should be easy to read, and the buttons should be clear.

After comparing the ideas, I felt the older adult social website was the most suitable one. It is not too small, but it is also not too broad. It gives us enough functions to build, but the overall direction is still clear.
